Smart Storage Tips

Discover expert tips and tricks for storing your ingredients properly. Learn how to extend shelf life, reduce waste, and maintain freshness.

Spinach

After washing, dry spinach thoroughly using a salad spinner or paper towels. Store in an airtight container lined with a dry paper towel in the fridge to prolong freshness.

Shelf Life: 5-7 days
üçì

Strawberries

Rinse strawberries only when ready to eat. Store them in a container lined with paper towels in the fridge to absorb moisture and prevent mold.

Shelf Life: 3-5 days
üßÄ

Cheddar Cheese

Wrap cheddar cheese in parchment paper and then loosely in aluminum foil or a container to allow some airflow while keeping moisture out.

Shelf Life: 3-4 weeks
üåø

Basil

Trim basil stems and place them in a glass with water, covering loosely with a plastic bag, or store at room temperature to maintain its fresh flavor.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
üçã

Lemons

Store in a sealed bag in the fridge to retain juiciness and prevent drying out.

Shelf Life: 2-3 weeks
üßÑ

Garlic

Keep in a dry, ventilated area away from sunlight to prevent sprouting.

Shelf Life: 3-5 months
üçÖ

Tomatoes

Store ripe tomatoes stem-side down at room temperature. Once ripe, refrigerate to slow down further ripening.

Shelf Life: 5-7 days
üçé

Apples

Store in the refrigerator crisper drawer. Keep away from other fruits as they release ethylene gas.

Shelf Life: 3-4 weeks
ü•õ

Milk

Keep in the coldest part of the refrigerator, not in the door. Return to fridge immediately after use.

Shelf Life: 5-7 days
üçó

Chicken

Store in the coldest part of the fridge, wrapped in its original packaging or in an airtight container.

Shelf Life: 1-2 days
üçå

Bananas

Store at room temperature until ripe, then refrigerate. Wrap stem ends in plastic to slow ripening.

Shelf Life: 5-7 days
ü•ï

Carrots

Remove green tops, store in a sealed bag in the crisper drawer. Keep away from ethylene-producing fruits.

Shelf Life: 2-3 weeks
ü•õ

Yogurt

Keep in the refrigerator at all times. Store in its original container and keep the lid tightly sealed.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
ü•©

Ground Beef

Store in the coldest part of the fridge. Use within 1-2 days or freeze for longer storage.

Shelf Life: 1-2 days
üçä

Oranges

Store at room temperature for up to a week, or in the refrigerator for longer storage.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
ü•¶

Broccoli

Store unwashed in a perforated plastic bag in the crisper drawer. Keep away from ethylene-producing fruits.

Shelf Life: 3-5 days
üçá

Grapes

Store unwashed in a perforated bag in the refrigerator. Wash only before eating.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
üßà

Butter

Store in the refrigerator door or butter compartment. Can be kept at room temperature for 1-2 days.

Shelf Life: 2-3 weeks
üêü

Salmon

Store in the coldest part of the fridge, wrapped in plastic wrap or in an airtight container.

Shelf Life: 1-2 days
ü´ë

Bell Peppers

Store unwashed in the crisper drawer. Keep away from ethylene-producing fruits.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
üçç

Pineapple

Store whole pineapple at room temperature until ripe, then refrigerate. Cut pineapple should be stored in an airtight container.

Shelf Life: 3-5 days
üßÄ

Cottage Cheese

Keep in the refrigerator at all times. Store in its original container and keep the lid tightly sealed.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
ü•©

Pork

Store in the coldest part of the fridge, wrapped in its original packaging or in an airtight container.

Shelf Life: 3-5 days

Mango

Store unripe mangoes at room temperature until ripe, then refrigerate. Cut mango should be stored in an airtight container.

Shelf Life: 4-7 days
ü•¨

Cauliflower

Store unwashed in a perforated plastic bag in the crisper drawer. Keep away from ethylene-producing fruits.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
ü•ù

Kiwi

Store unripe kiwis at room temperature until ripe, then refrigerate. Keep away from other fruits.

Shelf Life: 1-2 weeks
ü•õ

Sour Cream

Keep in the refrigerator at all times. Store in its original container and keep the lid tightly sealed.

Shelf Life: 2-3 weeks

Tuna

Store fresh tuna in the coldest part of the fridge, wrapped in plastic wrap or in an airtight container.

Shelf Life: 1-2 days

Shelf Life: 3-5 days

Shelf Life: 3-5 days

Cream Cheese

Keep in the refrigerator at all times. Store in its original container and keep the lid tightly sealed.

Shelf Life: 2-3 weeks
