üçì Strawberries

Rinse strawberries only when ready to eat. Store them in a container lined with paper towels in the fridge to absorb moisture and prevent mold.

Wrap cheddar cheese in parchment paper and then loosely in aluminum foil or a container to allow some airflow while keeping moisture out.

üåø Basil

üçã Lemons

üßÑ Garlic

ü•ë Avocados

Store unripe avocados at room temperature. Once ripe, refrigerate to slow spoilage. To keep cut avocados fresh, store with the pit and cover tightly.

ü•ï Carrots

Trim off greens and store carrots in a sealed container filled with water in the fridge to keep them crisp.

üçû Bread

Keep in a breathable cloth bag at room temperature to prevent mold. For longer storage, freeze sliced bread in an airtight bag.

ü•ö Eggs

üç† Potatoes

Store potatoes in a paper bag in a dark, cool, and well-ventilated place. Avoid refrigerating, as cold temperatures turn starches into sugar.

üßÖ Onions

Keep onions in a dark, cool place with good airflow. Do not store near potatoes, as they release gases that cause faster spoilage.

ü•´ Ketchup

Once opened, keep ketchup refrigerated with the cap sealed tightly to preserve its flavor and prevent spoilage.

ü•ú Peanut Butter

Natural peanut butter should be stored in the fridge to prevent separation, while commercial peanut butter can be kept at room temperature.

üçö Rice

Store uncooked rice in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Cooked rice should be refrigerated and consumed within 3-4 days.

üçó Chicken

Raw chicken should be stored on the bottom shelf of the fridge in its original packaging or an airtight container. Use within 1-2 days or freeze for longer storage.

üç£ Fish

Keep fresh fish in the coldest part of the fridge, ideally on a bed of ice in a sealed container. Use within 1-2 days or freeze for later use.

ü•© Beef

Raw beef should be stored in the fridge for up to 3 days. If freezing, wrap tightly in freezer paper or vacuum-seal to prevent freezer burn.

üçº Milk
